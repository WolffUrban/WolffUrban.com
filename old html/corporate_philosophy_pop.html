Philosophy
Maritz, Wolff’s mandate is three-fold: (1) purchase prime hotel and resort properties according to strict selection criteria; (2) work with the managers to improve the hotel’s bottom line performance, thereby producing attractive returns for their investors; and (3) re-invest strategically in physical assets. The partners believe that by working directly and cooperatively with a hotel’s management company to enhance revenues and reduce costs, they can maximize investment returns by adding significant value to properties.

From experience prior to forming Maritz, Wolff, Lew Wolff had an intimate knowledge of the hotel industry via his ownership of Hilton and Holiday Inn franchises in California, while Philip Maritz had a unique understanding of the marketing and sales issues facing top-tier hotel properties from his experience with Maritz Inc., a $2 billion, privately held, world leader in the incentive and corporate travel industry. Together, they established a series of unique principles that guide their decisions as to which properties to purchase and how to maximize the values inherent in each of their top-tier investments.

These key principles reflect both Maritz, Wolff’s business strategy as well as its competitive advantage:

  • Retain the existing management team, working cooperatively with them to enhance revenues and reduce costs.
  • Aggressively add value to the properties by applying a hand-on systematic approach to asset management.
  • Make strategic capital investments in portfolio hotels which yield attractive incremental returns.
