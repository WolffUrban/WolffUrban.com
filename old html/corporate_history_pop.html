History
The paths of Philip Maritz and Lew Wolff, both real estate investors/developers and St. Louis natives, crossed in 1992 and led to a breakfast meeting at The Ritz-Carlton Hotel in St. Louis, where they discussed collaborating on potential investment projects. Two years later they formed the hotel investment group, Maritz, Wolff & Co., and acquired The Ritz-Carlton, St. Louis. Following the investment, through an innovative approach to ownership, Maritz, Wolff worked with The Ritz-Carlton’s management team to increase the property’s revenues by 14 percent and operating profits by 127 percent. During this period, the partners observed factors in the hotel industry indicating strong investment opportunities for top-tier urban and resort lodging properties. This led to the creation of the Hotel Equity Fund, L.P. which today has nearly $500 million in invested equity capital and asset values in excess of $1.5 billion.

In October 1997, Maritz, Wolff acquired a 50 percent ownership in the five-star Mansion on Turtle Creek in Dallas and a 50 percent ownership in Rosewood Hotels and Resorts, LLC. Rosewood Hotels and Resorts is among the world’s premier luxury hotel management companies. Rosewood’s portfolio of luxury hotels and resorts includes award winning hotels; among them: The Carlyle, New York; The Mansion on Turtle Creek, Dallas; Las Ventanas, Los Cabos, Mexico; and Little Dix Bay, British Virgin Islands; The Seiyo Ginza, Tokyo.

In March 1998, Maritz, Wolff purchased a 50% interest in three more premier hotel properties -- The San Francisco Fairmont, The New Orleans Fairmont Hotel and The Dallas Fairmont Hotel. In addition to the hotel assets, Maritz, Wolff acquired a 50 percent stake in Fairmont Hotel Management Company, which manages landmark hotels including the Plaza Hotel, New York; Fairmont Copley Plaza, Boston; and Fairmont Hotels in San Jose and Chicago.

In April 1999, Maritz, Wolff & Co., with its partner Kingdom Hotels (USA) Ltd., on behalf of Fairmont Hotels Management LP, entered into an agreement with Canadian Pacific Limited and its wholly owned subsidiary, Canadian Pacific Hotels & Resorts, Inc. to create a new management company, Fairmont Hotels & Resorts, Inc. and to grow the Fairmont Hotel brand internationally.

The merger, finalized in October 1999, combined the management of two prestigious hotel groups and created a collection of luxury hotels across North America, Mexico and the Caribbean. The merger combined eight landmark Fairmont Hotels with 28 hotels from the Canadian Pacific Hotels which include the famous Chateau Frontenac, Banff Springs and Chateau Lake Louise. The new Fairmont management company also includes the Canadian Pacific-owned Princess resorts in Scottsdale, Arizona, Bermuda, Barbados and Acapulco. Following this transaction, Fairmont has added several prominent properties to its portfolio of managed hotels including: The Fairmont Kea Lani, Maui; The Fairmont Sonoma Mission Inn, Sonoma; The Fairmont Orchid, Hawaii; and The Fairmont, Washington D.C.
