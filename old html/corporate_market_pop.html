Market Opportunity
The extended down turn in the real estate and hotel industries during the late 1980s, early 1990s, and again in the early 2000s prompted many investors to steer clear of luxury hotels. Over-building, over-investment and over-financing by developers and lenders unfamiliar with the hotel industry created an oversupply of available hotel rooms resulting in reduced room rates, low occupancy levels and distressed properties. The depressed overall economy also caused pessimism in the hotel industry due to lack of available capital and lower property values.

In the midst of this declining hotel economy, Maritz and Wolff saw an opportunity to invest in and apply their value-added ownership approach to urban and resort hotel properties. Understanding that investment in under-performing, full-service luxury urban and resort hotel properties is most attractive just prior to a market’s recovery (while capital still is limited and competition is scarce), they designed their investment strategy to target selected top-tier properties to which they could add value at just the right time.

With the Hotel Equity Fund, Maritz, Wolff has acquired 18 luxury urban and resort hotels in the United States, Canada, Asia-Pacific and the Caribbean, with a current total of 6,000 hotel rooms generating in excess of $700 million in annual revenues. The portfolio of luxury hotels and resorts has a combined asset value in excess of $1.5 billion and is comprised of properties operated by Four Seasons, Ritz-Carlton, Fairmont, and Rosewood. Through active oversight and asset management, Maritz, Wolff has maximized cash flow while maintaining and enhancing long-term asset value. Improved bottom-lines at Maritz, Wolff owned assets have translated into attractive annual returns on investment (via cash distributions) for Maritz, Wolff investors, plus equity returns in excess of the firms targeted 25% IRR objective.
